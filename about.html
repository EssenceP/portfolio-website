About

My Story

Originally from Germany, I, now, currently reside in San Antonio, Texas. I became a UI Designer in hopes of discovering new ways to introduce tech to young minds. While teaching, I was exposed to the reality of students who were solely focused on memorizing information rather than learning due to the environment that had been created for them. I worked in a lower income area and quickly became aware of the lack of exposure to educational technology my students had. Rather than investing in quality computers and STEAM materials, TVs and bluetooth speakers were the selection of choice. To me, this conveyed that the children’s entertainment experience was prioritized much more than their learning experience. The turning point was when I had a student ask me how to use their imagination because they had never been taught before. It broke my heart. But rather than wallowing in the problem, I chose to become the solution.

I decided to make it my mission to find ways to positively influence the way young people think through design, tech, and creativity. It could go without saying that I’m very passionate about education, but I also have great interest in other topics like military well-being, diversity, and female empowerment. I try my best to create solutions for each of these areas through the work I create, which can be described as humanistic, solution-driven, and simplistic -- the three components that make me a 3-dimensional tech professional. When I’m not working, however, I enjoy being inspired through HGTV renovation shows, cooking without recipes, and researching the origin of everyday idioms (which all, ironically, end up contributing to my design process in one way or another).
